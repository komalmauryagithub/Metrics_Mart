1. INTRODUCTION

This HR Policy Manual outlines the employment rules, regulations, terms, conditions, responsibilities, workplace standards, and disciplinary framework governing employment with Metrics Mart Infoline Pvt Ltd (“Company”).

All employees are expected to read, understand, and comply with these policies during their tenure with the Company.

The Company reserves the right to amend, modify, update, or withdraw any policy at its sole discretion based on operational requirements, business needs, and applicable Indian laws.

2. SCOPE & APPLICABILITY

These policies shall apply to:

Permanent Employees

Probationary Employees

Contractual Employees

Interns & Trainees

Consultants

Freelancers engaged by the Company

Remote Employees

Technical & Non-Technical Staff

Specific terms may vary depending upon designation, department, appointment category, and employment agreement.

3. EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION

Employees may be categorized under the following classifications:

(A) Permanent Employees

Employees confirmed after successful completion of probation.

(B) Probationary Employees

Employees undergoing evaluation during initial employment tenure.

(C) Contractual Employees

Employees hired for fixed-duration assignments or projects.

(D) Interns/Trainees

Candidates undergoing training or internship programs.

(E) Consultants/Freelancers

External professionals engaged for specialized assignments.

4. RECRUITMENT & JOINING FORMALITIES

Employees shall submit all required documents at the time of joining including:

Educational certificates

PAN Card

Aadhaar Card

Passport-size photographs

Address proof

Bank account details

Previous employment documents

Experience certificates

Submission of false information, forged documents, or misrepresentation may result in immediate termination and legal action.

5. PROBATION POLICY

Every new employee shall undergo a probation period of 3 to 6 months from the date of joining.

During probation, the Company shall evaluate:

Performance

Attendance

Discipline

Work quality

Professional conduct

Learning ability

The Company reserves the right to:

Confirm employment

Extend probation

Terminate employment without assigning any reason subject to applicable law

Employees on probation may not be entitled to all benefits available to confirmed employees.

6. WORKING HOURS & ATTENDANCE POLICY

6.1 Working Hours

Standard working hours shall be 9 hours per day, including break time.

Employees may be required to work beyond standard hours depending upon business requirements.

6.2 Weekly Off

Sunday shall generally be the weekly off unless otherwise specified by management.

6.3 Attendance Rules

Employees must maintain regular and punctual attendance.

The following may attract disciplinary action:

Repeated late coming

Early leaving without approval

Unauthorized absenteeism

Habitual attendance irregularities

More than 3 late arrivals in a month may result in salary deduction or warning.

Absence without approval for more than 3 consecutive working days may be treated as abandonment of employment.

7. LEAVE & HOLIDAY POLICY

7.1 Annual Leave Entitlement

Leave TypeAnnual Entitlement
Casual Leave (CL)0 Days
Sick Leave (SL)1 Days
Earned/Privilege Leave (EL/PL)Days

7.2 Leave Rules

Leave must be approved in advance by the Reporting Manager.

Emergency leave must be informed immediately.

Sick Leave exceeding 2 consecutive days may require medical documentation.

Casual & Sick Leaves may lapse at year-end unless otherwise approved.

Earned Leave carry-forward shall be governed by Company policy.

Leave during notice period is generally not permitted.

7.3 Leave Without Pay (LWP)

Unauthorized absence or excess leave beyond entitlement shall be treated as Leave Without Pay (LWP).

Salary deduction shall apply proportionately.

8. SALARY, COMPENSATION & BENEFITS POLICY

8.1 Salary Structure

Compensation may include:

Basic Salary

House Rent Allowance (HRA)

Special Allowance

Incentives

Performance Bonus

PF Contributions

ESIC Benefits

Other statutory benefits where applicable

8.2 Salary Payment

Salaries shall generally be credited on or before the 7th working day of each month.

Salary slips shall be issued electronically.

8.3 Salary Deductions

Deductions may include:

Income Tax/TDS

PF/ESIC contributions

Leave Without Pay

Notice pay recovery

Loan/advance recovery

Damage caused to Company property

8.4 Incentives & Bonuses

Performance incentives and bonuses are discretionary and based on:

Employee performance

Business profitability

Client satisfaction

Department targets

No employee shall claim incentives as guaranteed compensation unless specifically stated in writing.

9. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY

Employee performance shall be reviewed periodically based on:

Work quality

Productivity

Attendance

Team collaboration

Professional behavior

Client feedback

Project completion

The Company may implement:

Performance Reviews

Appraisals

Warning Letters

Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

10. CODE OF CONDUCT & WORKPLACE ETHICS

Employees are expected to:

Maintain professionalism

Respect colleagues and clients

Follow lawful instructions

Protect Company assets

Maintain workplace discipline

Avoid conflicts of interest

The following are strictly prohibited:

Harassment

Discrimination

Abuse or violence

Fraudulent activity

Theft or dishonesty

Misuse of Company resources

11. ANTI-HARASSMENT & POSH POLICY

The Company strictly prohibits:

Sexual harassment

Verbal abuse

Workplace bullying

Gender discrimination

Mental harassment

The Company complies with:

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace

(Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (“POSH Act”)

Any complaint of harassment shall be investigated confidentially and disciplinary action may be initiated against the offender.

12. IT, INTERNET & DATA SECURITY POLICY

Company systems including:

Emails

Laptops

Servers

Databases

Internet connections

Software systems

shall be used strictly for official purposes only.

Employees shall not:

Access illegal or inappropriate content

Share confidential information externally

Install unauthorized software

Transfer Company data without approval

The Company reserves the right to monitor official systems and communications.

13. CONFIDENTIALITY & INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Employees shall maintain strict confidentiality regarding:

Client data

Source codes

Financial records

Credentials

Company strategies

Internal documents

Any work created during employment including:

Software

Designs

Reports

Marketing strategies

Technical documentation

shall remain the exclusive intellectual property of the Company.

Unauthorized disclosure or misuse may lead to termination and legal action.

14. REMOTE WORK / WORK FROM HOME POLICY

Where remote work is permitted:

Employees must remain available during official working hours.

Daily reporting or task tracking may be mandatory.

Confidentiality obligations shall continue during remote work.

Company devices and systems shall be used securely.

15. DISCIPLINARY ACTION POLICY

Disciplinary action may include:

Verbal Warning

Written Warning

Salary deduction

Suspension

Termination

Serious misconduct may result in immediate termination without notice.

16. RESIGNATION & NOTICE PERIOD POLICY

16.1 Employee Resignation

Confirmed employees shall serve 30–60 days notice period.

Probationary employees shall serve 15 days notice period.

16.2 Notice Period Buyout

The Company may permit buyout of notice period at its sole discretion.

Equivalent salary for the unserved period may be recovered.

16.3 Termination by Company

The Company reserves the right to terminate employment for:

Poor performance

Policy violation

Misconduct

Fraud

Absenteeism

Confidentiality breach

Business restructuring

Termination may be with or without notice depending upon the severity and applicable law.

17. FULL & FINAL SETTLEMENT

Upon separation:

Employees must return all Company property.

Clearance formalities must be completed.

Recoveries, damages, advances, or notice pay may be adjusted.

Final settlement shall be processed as per Company policy and applicable law.

18. NON-COMPETE & NON-SOLICITATION

Employees shall not directly or indirectly:

Solicit Company clients

Hire Company employees

Use Company contacts for personal business

Compete using Company confidential knowledge

for a period of 12 months after separation from employment.

19. APPLICABLE INDIAN LABOUR & EMPLOYMENT LAWS

This policy shall be governed by applicable Indian laws including but not limited to:

(A) Shops & Establishments Act

Applicable state-wise for:

Working hours

Leave rules

Employee welfare

Holidays

(B) Payment of Wages Act, 1936

Applicable for lawful salary payment and deductions.

(C) Employees’ Provident Funds & Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952

Applicable for PF contributions where eligible.

(D) Employees’ State Insurance Act, 1948

Applicable for medical and insurance benefits where eligible.

(E) Payment of Bonus Act, 1965

Applicable for statutory bonus provisions.

(F) Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972

Applicable upon completion of qualifying service.

(G) Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Applicable in matters relating to employment disputes and termination.

(H) Information Technology Act, 2000

Applicable in cases involving:

Data misuse

Unauthorized access

Cyber misconduct

Confidentiality breaches

(I) Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023

Applicable in cases involving:

Fraud

Criminal breach of trust

Misappropriation

Financial misconduct
